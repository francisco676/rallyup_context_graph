The Core Idea

What Is a Context Graph?

A context graph is a set of structured markdown files that describe who you are, what you know, and what your company sells. No fancy tools, no subscriptions. Just organized information any AI can read.

In the age of AI, good context = good content. The prompt is almost irrelevant. The context is everything.

When you hand a generic prompt to Claude, you get generic content. When you hand it a context graph, it knows your expertise, your voice, your audience, your goals, and what you should never say. The output stops sounding like AI and starts sounding like you.

The Architecture

The 3 Layers of a Strong Context Graph

Every effective context graph is built in three layers. Each one makes the AI output sharper.

L1
Professional Identity
Your role, your expertise, your ideal audience, and your LinkedIn goals. This is the foundation. Without it, the AI does not know who it is writing for or why.
L2
Voice DNA
Your writing style, tone, sentence patterns, words you use, words you avoid. This is what makes your content sound like you instead of like every other executive on LinkedIn.
L3
Content Library
Past posts, what performed well, ongoing themes. The AI gets smarter every time you add a post to this layer. Your best content trains future content.

The Build Guide

Step-by-Step: Build Your Context Graph in 45 Minutes

Open Claude (or any AI assistant) and work through these five steps in order. Each step gives you a prompt you can paste directly. Do not skip steps. Each file builds on the ones before it.

Step 1 Your Company Context (15 min) — 5 files

Paste this into Claude with your company name and website. It will research your public presence and create five structured files. Flag anything marked [NEEDS VERIFICATION] and fix it manually.

Prompt to paste into Claude
I need you to research my company and create 5 structured files I can save as markdown. For each file, include a section called "Connected Nodes" at the bottom with links to the other files using this format: [[filename]] My company is: [YOUR COMPANY NAME] Our website is: [YOUR WEBSITE URL] Create these 5 files: 1. positioning.md — value prop, 3-4 differentiators, how you present publicly Connected Nodes: [[icps]], [[product]], [[competitors]], [[trends]] 2. icps.md — 2-3 ideal customer profiles, their pain, what they care about Connected Nodes: [[positioning]], [[product]], [[trends]] 3. product.md — products/services, features, who uses them Connected Nodes: [[positioning]], [[icps]], [[competitors]] 4. competitors.md — 3-5 competitors, where you win, where they win Connected Nodes: [[positioning]], [[product]], [[trends]] 5. trends.md — 5-7 current trends, why each matters for your company Connected Nodes: [[positioning]], [[competitors]] Label anything uncertain as [NEEDS VERIFICATION]. Format each file with: --- created: [today's date] ---

Save into: company/ and market/ folders.

Step 2 Your Voice (10 min) — the most important file

This file teaches AI how you sound. If you have 10 or more LinkedIn posts, paste your five best alongside this prompt. If you do not have posts yet, use the alternative prompt at the bottom.

Prompt to paste into Claude (with posts)
I'm going to share some of my LinkedIn posts. Analyze my writing voice and create a brand-voice.md file that captures how I sound. Include: - Core Identity: who I am professionally (1-2 sentences) - Voice Archetype: one sentence (e.g. "The Observational Strategist") - DO list: 8-10 things I consistently do in my writing - DON'T list: 8-10 things I avoid - Signature Phrases: 5-10 direct quotes from the posts - Vocabulary: 10-15 words I use, 10-15 to avoid - How I Open Posts: pattern + 3 examples - How I Close Posts: pattern + 3 examples Connected Nodes: [[guardrails]], [[sales-focus]], [[topic-1]] Here are my posts: [PASTE YOUR 5-10 BEST LINKEDIN POSTS HERE]
Alternative prompt if you have no posts yet
I want to build a LinkedIn presence. Help me define my brand voice. Ask me these questions one at a time, then create a brand-voice.md file: 1. What's your role and what makes your perspective unique? 2. When you explain something at work, how do people describe your style? 3. Think of 2-3 LinkedIn creators whose tone you admire. Who are they? 4. What kind of posts make you cringe? 5. Are you more: data-driven or story-driven? Formal or casual? Teaching or sharing?

Save as: voice/brand-voice.md

Step 3 Your Expertise Topics (10 min) — 2-3 concept files

These files define the topics you are credible to write about. Each one becomes a content engine that the AI can draw from when building posts.

Prompt to paste into Claude
Based on my role as [YOUR ROLE] at [YOUR COMPANY], I want to define 2-3 content topics to post about on LinkedIn. For each topic, create a separate markdown file with: - Definition: what this topic means to me (2-3 sentences) - What It Means: 3-4 bullet points on what I believe - What It Doesn't Mean: 2-3 misconceptions to avoid - Company Connection: how this topic relates to what my company does - Content Ideas: 5 specific post ideas Connected Nodes: [[brand-voice]], [[positioning]], [[sales-focus]] My expertise areas are: [TOPIC 1]: [one sentence description] [TOPIC 2]: [one sentence description]

Save as: concepts/topic-name.md (use a descriptive name, e.g. sales-leadership.md)

Step 4 Guardrails and Goals (5 min) — 2 quick files

Two short files that protect your voice and keep content on strategy. Guardrails tell the AI what to avoid. Sales-focus tells it what actually matters to you.

Prompt to paste into Claude
Create two files for my LinkedIn content strategy: 1. guardrails.md Topics I should never post about: - [List 3-5 things to avoid — e.g. politics, competitor bashing, hustle culture] Content patterns that underperform on LinkedIn: - Generic advice without a specific story - Posts that end with "What do you think?" (engagement bait) - Abstract trend predictions without personal experience - Pure self-promotion disguised as insight Connected Nodes: [[brand-voice]], [[sales-focus]] 2. sales-focus.md My LinkedIn goals, ranked: 1. [YOUR #1 GOAL — e.g. build personal authority, generate leads] 2. [YOUR #2 GOAL] Content priorities (in order): 1. [e.g. real stories from work] 2. [e.g. industry observations] Connected Nodes: [[brand-voice]], [[guardrails]], [[topic-1]]

Save as: concepts/guardrails.md and concepts/sales-focus.md

Step 5 The Master Index (5 min) — the file that ties everything together

This file tells any AI exactly how to use your graph when you ask it to write a post. Think of it as the operating manual for your context system.

Prompt to paste into Claude
Create a master index file called my-context-graph.md. Include: Identity: One paragraph about who I am (name, role, company, what makes me distinct) Node Map: A list of every file with a one-line description and a wikilink. Group into: Voice / Concepts / Company / Market Format: - [[filename]] — one line description Execution Instructions: A numbered list telling AI how to use this graph when writing a post: 1. Check [[guardrails]] first 2. Find a specific story or observation to anchor the post 3. Match the topic to a concept node 4. Read [[brand-voice]] for voice rules 5. Read [[sales-focus]] to check which goal this serves 6. Write in my voice — open with experience, let insight emerge 7. End with a declaration or reflection, not a question Connected Nodes: (link to every file)

Save as: my-context-graph.md in the root folder.

Using Your Graph

How to Write a Post With Your Context Graph

Once your files are built, paste this master prompt into Claude with the relevant files attached. Even 3 files will produce dramatically better output than a blank prompt.

The master content prompt
Read all the files in my context graph (pasted below). Then write a LinkedIn post about [YOUR TOPIC]. Follow the execution instructions in my-context-graph.md. Match my voice from brand-voice.md. Check guardrails.md before writing. [PASTE: my-context-graph.md] [PASTE: brand-voice.md] [PASTE: guardrails.md] [PASTE: relevant concept file]

Tips That Make the Graph Compound Over Time

  • Update your graph quarterly. Trends change. Your goals shift. Your voice evolves. Set a reminder every 90 days.
  • Save every post you publish to the content/ folder. Dated markdown files. Each post trains future content.
  • The graph compounds. The first post will be good. The tenth will be indistinguishable from you.
  • Share it with your team. Multiple people at your company can share the same company/ and market/ files and only need individual voice and concept files.
  • Use Obsidian to visualize your graph. Open your folder as a vault, press Cmd+G, and you will see every file as a node and every [[wikilink]] as a connection.
